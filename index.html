Silicon Level Forecasting using LSTM

Predicting Si Levels in Rivers for Marine Diatoms' Survival

Abstract

This project aims to forecast silicon (Si) levels in rivers using LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) networks. Silicon is a crucial nutrient for marine diatoms, essential for one-fourth of global primary production. Our LSTM model analyzes aquatic, ecological, and climatic parameters to predict Si levels, offering insights into nutrient processing in rivers.

Accomplishment

  • Developed an LSTM-based model to predict Si levels from time-series data.
  • Integrated climate and ecological variables to enhance prediction accuracy.
  • Provided valuable insights for marine ecosystem conservation and climate change mitigation.
  • Explored the idea of having historical significance in the Si values through time-series setup.

Research Motivation

Climate change is disrupting nutrient cycles, making accurate predictions vital for maintaining ecosystem balance. This project fills a gap by applying LSTM networks to predict riverine Si levels, which is essential for sustaining marine life, especially diatoms.



Ethical Analysis of Media Recommenders

Social and Ethical Implications of Algorithm-Driven Media Recommendations

Executive Summary

With the rapid development of technology, algorithmic recommendation systems have become crucial to online media consumption. This project explores the ethical complexities of these systems, focusing on platforms like Netflix, YouTube, Instagram, and Reddit. These algorithms enhance user experience but also pose ethical challenges, particularly regarding their influence on users, content creators, and society.

Accomplishment

  • Analyzed ethical concerns of personalization algorithms on social media and streaming platforms.
  • Explored accountability in AI-based systems, discussing issues like algorithmic transparency and user impact.
  • Provided recommendations for policymakers and developers to mitigate ethical risks.
  • Reviewed novel techniques like collaborative filtering and attention mechanisms to improve system explainability.

Research Focus

This project calls for greater accountability and ethical reflection on media recommendation systems. By emphasizing transparency and user control, the research highlights the need for balancing innovation with responsibility in the digital age.
